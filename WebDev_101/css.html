Content

Today I would like to talk about something that becomes an essential part of our day-to-day routine - the web and how to create a website. I believe many of us were buying some product online, ordering some service, or just looking up a piece of information. Some of us could be a business owner and again to reach out to the costumers it would be beneficial to be online present.


So, what is the recipe for creating a website? First and foremost, lets us answer the question “what is the internet?”. According to the Wikipedia website “the internet is the global system of interconnected computer networks that uses the Internet protocol suite (TCP/IP) to communicate between networks and devices”. Furthermore, if one wishes to have a website then it requires a domain name which will be an address on the internet. There are plenty of providers where you can buy one, also those companies provide hosting service. The next step is to choose technologies and tools, and here all depends on the aim of the website. The first way is creating it from scratch and for that, you at least need to know programming languages like HTML, CSS, JavaScript which are the pillars of the web per se. It is not necessary to go to a university to learn all this. There are plenty of free sources and the IT community is vast, friendly, and supportive. I would like to highlight one sufficient source, it is w3school.com which is free training website for learning web technologies online. Besides the certificate which you can claim after completing the course, you can start applying for a modern job as a web developer. However, if all this sound too complicated and you just want to set up quickly a web page for your small business than there is good news for you. In the past few years were created many CMS's (Content Management System's) like WordPress, Wix, etc which allowed to set up a web page without any bits of knowledge in coding. After a few hours of a tutorial, you will be able to create a nice, responsive website which will be tailored for your business needs.

In the end, I would like to say that ether way of creating a web site could be challenging but it also could be very rewarding and satisfying to see your creation online.
